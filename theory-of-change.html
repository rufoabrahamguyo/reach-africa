Theory of Change

“Sustainable community transformation occurs when families, children, and communities are empowered with knowledge, resources, and opportunities while systems that support livelihoods, health, education, and the environment are strengthened.”

If vulnerable communities gain access to quality education, child development support, sustainable livelihoods, improved health systems, and responsible environmental stewardship, then they will be better equipped to overcome poverty, hunger, and vulnerability, resulting in resilient communities where children flourish and families achieve sustainable well-being. This transformation is achieved through partnerships with communities, faith institutions, government agencies, civil society, and the private sector.

Theory of Change Flow

Inputs

  • Partnerships
  • Technical expertise
  • Community engagement
  • Funding and resources
→

Activities

  • Education and child development programs
  • Livelihood and agricultural support
  • Health and One Health initiatives
  • Environmental conservation
  • Community leadership development
→

Outputs

  • Children accessing education and care
  • Farmers and families improving livelihoods
  • Communities adopting healthier practices
  • Improved natural resource management
→

Outcomes

  • Increased household income
  • Improved health and nutrition
  • Stronger family and community resilience
  • Sustainable environmental practices
→

Impact

  • Resilient communities, flourishing children, and sustainable well-being across Africa.
